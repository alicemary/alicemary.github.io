User-centred Design Approach

An essential part of my workflow is gathering user data which allows for well-informed design decisions based on research. With the availabity of so much user information these days, many things can be established such as click-through rate, the most commonly used devices and the frequency of specific Google searches. Likewise, observation and interviews go a long way to help uncover how people are interacting with the piece and their level of understanding.

About Alice

Web Design and Medical Drawings

For the last several years I have developed my skills in web development by creating websites, learning programming, mastering Adobe and studying design. This went hand-in-hand with my artistic abilities which I set out to elevate to a professional level through practice, mentorship and study. Now I create scientifically accurate graphics and work periodically with members of The Faculty of Dentistry, University of Toronto to inform students and other interested parties of their research, activities and goals.

Medical Science Background

Bachelor of Medical Science with First Class Honours in Medicine from The University of Western Australia

Bachelor of Science with majors in Microbiology and Pathology from The University of Western Australia

During my studies I worked at The Marshall Centre for Infectious Diseases investigating pathogenicity islands in Shigella. I then completed my thesis at the Institute for Respiratory Health where I researched cell signalling pathways and other mechanisms driving fibrotic lung disease. After moving to Toronto, Canada, my research into fibrosis continued at The Faculty of Dentistry, University of Toronto.
